I am a PhD candidate in the Department of Government at Harvard University. I specialize in Chinese politics, with a focus on China's political economy in historical and international perspectives. My research utilizes both statistical analysis and qualitative methods to examine the relationship between the Chinese Communist Party and the private sector. I additionally write on Chinese trade practices and behavior at the WTO, the reform era in China, and the US-China economic relationship.


Before beginning my PhD studies, I was the Princeton-in-Asia Fellow at Ashinaga Ikueikai in Tokyo, Japan. I have also held internships focusing on Asia policy at the US Department of State in Washington, DC, the US Embassy in Beijing, and at the Center for American Progress.
